GSMST
Computer Science Club

The CS Club of Georgia's #1 High School, made by students for students

Area of Interests

Cybersecurity

In CS club, we learn about cybersecurity and how to protect ourselves on the internet. We study system administration, network security, virtualization, and cloud computing on Windows, Linux, and Cisco.

Algorithm

We learn about common algorithms and data structures, and how to implement them in Java, Python, and C++. We practice our skills by solving problems on LeetCode and Codeforces.

Game design

We delve into the realm of ubiquitous algorithmic methodologies and data organization schemes, and how to implement them utilizing Java, Python, and C++. We hone our aptitudes by resolving conundrums on LeetCode and Codeforces.

Programming/Girl's Who Code

We hold symposia and hands-on sessions to instruct students in the art of Python coding. From variable declaration to object-oriented programming, web development, artificial intelligence/machine learning, and event-driven programming, we encompass it all.

Competitions

CyberPatriot

CyberPatriot is a national high school and middle school cyber defense competition created by the Air Force Association to inspire students toward careers in cybersecurity or other science, technology, engineering, and mathematics (STEM) disciplines critical to our nation's future.

PicoCTF

PicoCTF is a free computer security game targeted at middle and high school students, created by Carnegie Mellon University's Software Engineering Institute.

USACO

The United States of America Computing Olympiad (USACO) is a competitive programming competition for high school students.

CodeQuest

CodeQuest is a programming competition for high school students in Georgia. It is hosted by Lockhead Martin.

Games for Change

Games for Change is a non-profit organization that connects game developers and educators to create social impact through games.
